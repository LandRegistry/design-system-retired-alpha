Accessibility alpha ideas for an HMLR design system

How to make digital services that work for everyone.

Your service must be accessible to everyone who needs it, the public, professionals and staff. If it is not, you may be breaking the law.

HM Land Registry lawyers do not believe that when creating new or revising existing services that we can claim that complying with accessibility regualtions would cause us a disproportionate burden - so all new and revised services should meet them. How to find out more about our legal position

Everyone who works on digital services has a role to play in making them accessible and inclusive.

The patterns, components, styles and content in this manual have been tested for accessibility and are WCAG 2.1 compliant, unless stated otherwise. However, assembling the parts into a service does not make it accessible.

As well as the guides here, the accessibility community of interest (which includes members from many communites of practice, including user research, product, design, test, front end development) can help you with creating accessible services and testing them
